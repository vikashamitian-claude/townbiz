## CHAPTER 1 — "Save My First Shop"  *(fully detailed)*

**Chapter question:** *Can you turn a tiny shop into a steady profit?*
**Start:** ₹10,000, one rented shop, one product (Soap), no employee, no experience — a **Dreamer**.
**This chapter IS the playable prototype.** Exact numbers, demand model, and trait tags live in
[PROTOTYPE_SPEC.md](PROTOTYPE_SPEC.md) §4–§7; below is the mission-level view.

---

### Mission 1 — Opening Day
- **❓ Question:** *Can you price so people buy — and you still profit?*
- **📈 Concept:** Pricing & margin (Financial)
- **🧠 Mindset (earned):** **Initiative** — you set your own price; you stake your claim. No one tells you the number.
- **Trigger:** Nobody knows your shop. Your shelf of soap is stocked. *What's your price?*
- **Decision:** the **margin dial** — drag price, watch demand & projected profit respond live; lock it, run 5 days.
- **Consequence:** your first earnings build your reserve; customers smile or walk past.
- **Lesson:** *"Price too high, customers leave. Too low, you work for free. Profit lives in the middle."*
- **Advances:** Dreamer → **Shopkeeper**.

### Mission 2 — Running Out of Stock
- **❓ Question:** *Can you keep up with demand without drowning in stock?*
- **📈 Concept:** Inventory (read demand; overstock freezes cash, understock loses sales)
- **🧠 Mindset (earned):** **Foresight / responsibility** — you must plan ahead, not just react.
- **Trigger:** Soap is nearly gone and customers keep asking. The supplier offers a bulk discount.
- **Decision:** small order (safe) · large bulk order (cheaper, ties up cash) · emergency buy (fast, premium).
- **Consequence:** demand is clearly high — bulk pays off, but the player *feels* their cash lock into the shelf.
- **Lesson:** *"Empty shelves lose customers. Too much stock locks your cash. Order to match demand."*

### Mission 3 — The Long Queue *(centerpiece)*
- **❓ Question:** *Can you trust someone else with your dream?*
- **📈 Concept:** Delegation (HR) — you grow only as far as your own two hands
- **🧠 Mindset (earned):** **Trust** — you literally hand a stranger your till; trust earns loyalty, control stalls growth.
- **Trigger:** a queue builds; you tap to serve but **can't keep up** — customers leave. Then **Ravi** appears: *"Sir… do you need help?"*
- **Decision:** **Hire Ravi** (−₹150/day) · raise prices to thin the crowd · push through alone.
- **Consequence (if hired):** Ravi serves a customer *you* used to serve. *"Thank you for the chance, boss."*
  The shop runs without your hands — the first **"it ran without me"** beat. (Seeds the future mirror.)
- **Lesson:** *"You can only grow as far as your own two hands — until you trust someone else's."*
- **Advances:** Shopkeeper → **Employer**.

### Mission 4 — Month-End *(tests the reserve)*
- **❓ Question:** *Can you survive your first month?*
- **📈 Concept:** Cash flow (Financial) — profit on paper isn't money in hand
- **🧠 Mindset (earned):** **Discipline / responsibility** — the bills come whether you're ready or not.
- **Trigger:** the month closes. **Rent ₹3,000** and **Ravi's wages** are due. *Can you cover it?*
- **Decision:** pay everything on time · take a small loan · delay the supplier. (Broke → harder path, **never game-over**.)
- **Consequence:** reserve covers it → relief & pride; short → you feel exactly why margin discipline mattered.
- **Lesson:** *"Profit on paper isn't money in hand. Cash flow keeps the doors open."*
- **Advances:** Employer → **Business Owner**.

### Mission 5 — The Shop Next Door  *(chapter summit)*
- **❓ Question:** *Can you grow without collapsing?*
- **📈 Concept:** Investment & expansion (Strategy)
- **🧠 Mindset (earned):** **Courage** — a big, hard-to-reverse bet with real downside; safety means slower growth.
- **Trigger:** the shop next door is available. Doubling your space needs a big investment (~₹8,000).
- **Decision:** expand now (shop doubles) · stay small for now (a proud, smaller summit).
- **Consequence → Completion screen:** *"You built your first successful business."* Growth summary
  (Cash · Net Worth · Customers · Employees · Reputation) + a **mirror line** reading your trait
  fingerprint + the hook: **Next → Chapter 2: Winning Customers.**
- **Lesson:** *"Growth is a bet. Bet what you can survive losing."*
