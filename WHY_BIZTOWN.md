# Why BizTown Exists

*This document answers one question, and only one. It never changes.
Read it before you touch BizTown — as a developer, artist, writer, designer, marketer, or investor.*

---

Across the world, millions of young people dream of building something of their own — a shop, a
brand, a company, an empire. They are ambitious, curious, and capable. But they have nowhere to
*learn* how.

Business is taught to them as theory: dry textbooks, formulas, lectures they forget. Or it is
learned the hard way — through real money, real failure, real fear. One path is boring. The other
is brutal. Neither is built for a dreamer taking their first step.

**BizTown exists to give them a third path.**

A place to *live* the entrepreneur's journey before they risk a single rupee. To start with nothing,
make real decisions, feel real consequences, fail safely, and try again. To learn how a business
actually grows — pricing, people, cash, risk, trust — not by being told, but by doing.

But BizTown is not really about business.

It is about the person the player becomes along the way. With every decision they change — learning
to take initiative, to trust others, to stay disciplined, to act with courage, to think long-term.
They start as a **Dreamer** and become an **Empire Builder** — not because the game hands them a
title, but because they earned it.

That is the heart of BizTown, and the one sentence that captures everything:

> ## BizTown is not about becoming rich. It is about becoming capable.

The money, the buildings, the factories, the brands — they are not the point. They are *evidence*.
Evidence of capability. **And capability is the real progression.**

If, after hundreds of hours, a young person closes BizTown and thinks not *"I have more money"* but
**"I think differently now — I could actually build something"** — then BizTown has done its job.

That is why BizTown exists. Everything else is detail.
